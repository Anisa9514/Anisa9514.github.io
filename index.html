About Me

Me in a nutshell

I am a graduating biomedical engineering major at the University of Texas at Austin with a passion for web development and currently looking for full-time opportunities in software with an amphasis on web programming.

My Path

Biomedical engineering => Web Development ?

My fascination with biology led me to choose biomedical engineering as my major. I quickly learned, however, that while learning the theory was great, I was not a big fan of lab work

Luckily for me, biomedical engineering covers a lot of disciplines, one of which is software. I quickly developed an interest in programming. I loved the ability to solve a problem needing only a computer and some creative thinking.

My Path Continued

I then took a software engineering co-op where I got involved in some projects involving web development, and I was hooked. A lot of what I did focused on front end, which I felt was the perfect combination of design and programming.

It was when I started spending most of my free time looking up resources on web development that I realized this is the career path I wanted to go to, and I have not looked back since.

Experience

Software Engineering Co-op

Abbott (Formerly St. Jude Medical)

June 2017 - December 2017 (6.5 months)

Abbott is a top healthcare company with a broad range of products from diagnostics to nutrition to medical devices. I worked in their neuromodulation division in Plano. My department handled the manufacture of pulse generators that send controlled bursts of stimulation to nerves within the body to alleviate diseases like chronic pain. These generators are configured through an iOS app that communicate with the surgically implanted generators through bluetooth.

Throughout my co-op, I had been involved in projects with different teams within the department:

  • I worked with the firmware team to automate their manual test suite, increasing productivity by allowing their tests to be run overnight without manual intervention (C Programming, IVI Drivers)
  • I worked with the software testing team to automate verification testing for the iOS neuromodulation app. (C# Programming, Ranorex Test Automation, Visual Basic)
  • Within the co-op team, I was in charge of maintaining a task manager web application that we use to keep track of our weekly sprints (HTML, CSS, Javascript, PHP, SQL)
  • My main project involved creating the front end web interface for an equipment tracker application. The app would make it easier to account for where resources are allocated within the department. (HTML, CSS, Javascript, Vue, Vuex)
